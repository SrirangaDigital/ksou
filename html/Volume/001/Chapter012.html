  • Further a lumpsum of Rs. 2,000 per annum is provided for the supply of clothes to Depressed Class non- scholarship-holders of which Rs. 1,600 per annum is for boys and Rs. 400 per annum is for girls.
  • Student who once fails to secure promotion from one class to another will be given reduced scholarship, the reduction amounting to not less than one - third of the ordinary value of the scholarship, according to the manner in which the candidates have acquitted themselves in their examinations. Ordinary no student who fails twice will be eligible for a scholarship, and, in no case , a student who has failed thrice.
  • As far as possible, scholars under this scheme will be required to live in a recognized boarding establishment, whether government or aided where such exists and the scholarships due to them will be handed over to the managers of the institutions to be expended on their behalf, proper accounts maintained in all case by the said manages.
  • With a view to inculcate habits of tidiness among the pupils of Depressed Classes:
    1. the scholarship amounts of the student in Primary III and IV Middle School Classes will be reduced by annas 8 per mensem and the sum so reduced (Rs. 5 for 10 months) will be utilized for the supply of clothing to the scholars. Similarly the whole of the scholarship in the Primary I and II year classes at Re. 0-8-0 per mensem will be utilized for the supply of clothing and it will not be paid in cash while the pupils of the high School and higher classes are expected to provide themselves with decent clothes out of their scholarships.
    2. In the case of non- scholarship holders, a sum not exceeding Rs. 5 each will, where necessary, be paid by the Inspector of Primary and Middle Schools in the case of boys and Inspectress in the case of girls towards the clothing of the student for which purpose a Lumpsum provision of Rs. 2,000 has been provided during the year as stated in para 1above
  • In all the above cases, the Inspector of Middle and Primary School or Inspectress, as the case may be, will draw the clothing allowance i,e., Rs 5 each in advance and order out a set of uniform clothing for each student the cost being within the clothing allowance sanctioned, and distribute the clothes to the students concerned in light of the instruction issued in this Office No. cir 8 of 16th October 1923. Any change remaining after this supply may be paid to them in cash. In the case of scholarship holders, the signature of students being taken in the salary acquittance roll of the school, both for the supply of clothing and the payment of cash, in the case of scholarship holders and for the supply of clothing in the case of non-scholarship-holders.
  • Applications in the prescribed form other printed or manuscript from students of Primary and Middle Schools must, in the case of boys, be submitted to the district Inspectors through their Headmasters and from those of High Schools including Junior Intermediate classes to the Inspector of Secondary and Normal Schools not later than 1st July. the applications of girls of all grades whether reading in Boys'or Girls' Schools should, however, be sent to the Inspectress not later than the same date. the District Inspectors will consolidate the applications received, the names of students being arranged in the order of merit and submit the same to the Inspector of Middle and primary Schools not later than 7th July. the Inspectors and Inspectress of Girls' Schools, as the case may be, will award the above scholarships and submit a copy of the award list to the head office with full particulars as to the amount utilized, etc.
