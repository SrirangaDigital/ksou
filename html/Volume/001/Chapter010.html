1925 Part 1 - Documents

Document No. (1)

Backward Class Scholarships (Published on Vol 60, No 47, November 21, 1925 p. 299-300) READ-

Correspondence ending with letter No. H. C. 81 of 25-26, dated 26th September 1925, from the Inspector-General of Education, submitting proposals for the utilization of the additional allotment of 8,000 in the current year's budget for Backward Class Scholarships and stating that a separate communication will be made in regard to the utilization of the amount for the next year.

OrderNo.

ORDER No. E. 2483-85-EDN. 148-23-9, 13th NOVEMVER 1925

the Inspector- General proposes to allot the sum of Rs. 8,000 among the several kinds of scholarships on the population basis as noted in the margin and to further distribute the amount of Rs. 6,071 under item (a) among the several groups of communities under Backward Classes in proportion to their population and in all the three grades.

OrderNo.

Rs.
(a) Backward Class scholarships (excluding
Mahomadan and Depressed Class scholarships) … … 6,071
(b) Mahomadan Scholarships … … 427
(c) Depressed Class Scholarships, English Classes … 1,502
Rs. 8,000
  • HE also states that if proportion of the amount is intended to be given to the University, the allotments for the several kinds of scholarships will be proportionately reduced.
  • He states further that as the amount available is too small to be divided among the several District and Taluk Ad Hoc Committees, the same will be utilized for pupils of the particular communities along with lapsed scholarships.
  • Government observe that the claims of students in the University cannot altogether be overlooked and accordingly direct that a lump sum of Rs. 750 out of the allotment of Rs. 8,000 be placed at the disposal of the University for award of scholarships to students belonging to backward communities in the University and the balance utilized in the Education Department. the Inspector-General is requested to distribute the amount among the various kinds of scholarships and the several communities on the basis of population and award the scholarships in the manner suggested by him during the current year. His proposals for the utilization of the amount from next year inwards will be awaited.

P.G. D'SOUZA
Chief Secretary to Government
