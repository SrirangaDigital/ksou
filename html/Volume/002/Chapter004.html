Ministerial Service (Clerk's Guide) Examination, 1944

An examination for recruitment to the Ministerial Service (Clerk's Guide) of the Government of India will be held at Allahabad, Bombay, Calcutta, Delhi, Lahore and Madras, on the 26th February 1944. Candidates must have passed the Matriculation or Cambridge School Certificate or an equivalent examination and must be between the ages of 17 and 19 (or 17 and 22 in the case of Scheduled Castes candidates) on the 1st July 1943. Last date for the receipts of applications is 24th December 1943. Application forms and full particulars obtainable from the Secretary, Federal Public Service Commission, Simla.

Document No. (4)

No.R.L.261-R.A. 2-44-2, dated 21st -26th July 1944

Whereas Mr. Muniyappa, Municipal Councillor, residing at Harijancheri, Chickballapur, elected to the Representative Assembly, for the Depressed Class reserved seat at the bye-election held in accordance with Notification No. Rl. 1495-R.A.38-41-1, dated 11th February 1942, from the Goribidnur General Rural Constituency, incurred disqualification under Section 20(1) (h) of the Government of Mysore Act, 1940, and under rule 195 of the Representative Assembly Rules 1940, for failure to lodge a return of election expenses within the time prescribed;

And whereas by their Order No. RI.713-14-R.A.10-42-10, dated 6th January 1943, the Government were pleased to direct, in exercise of the powers vested in them under proviso to clause (h) of sub-section (1) of Section 20 of the Act, that the disqualification incurred under Section 20 (I)(h) of the aforesaid Act, by Mr. Muniyappa for being chosen as and for being a member of either chamber, shall take effect from the 13th day of June 1942;

And whereas the said Mr. Muniyappa has presented a petition to Government praying for the removal of the disqualification from being a voter for five years for the reasons set out in the petition;

Now, therefore, in exercise of the powers vested in them under the provisions of rule 197 of the Representative Assembly Rules, 1940, the Government of His Highness the Maharaja are hereby pleased to direct that the disqualification incurred by Mr. Muniyappa under rule 195 of the aforesaid rules to vote at any election, to either Chamber for a period of five years shall cease to have effect from the 10th day of July 1944.
