Order No. E. 3346-9-Edn. 36-23-7, dated 23rd January, 1924

1. Primary stage 250 2 6,000

Grand Total … … 1,00,000
  • The tenure of these scholarships having been fixed at ten months in the year as in the case of the other local scholarships as a measure of retrenchment in accordance with Government Order No. 1875-82-Edn. 134-21-57, dated the 9th September 1922, the allotment for the Backward Classes Scholarships was reduced to Rs. 83,300 i.e., by Rs. 16,700 with effect from 1922-23. A representation having however been made in the Representative Assembly during the Birthday Session in June 1923, for the restoration of the provision for the Backward Classes scholarships to the original sum of one lakh of rupees by the addition of the amount withdrawn as a result of reduction in the tenure of the scholarships, an additional sum of Rs. 17,000 has been included in the budget estimates of the Education Department for the current year.
  • In regard to the utilization of the above supplemental grant of Rs. 17,000, the Inspector-General of Education has proposed that in view of the large number of applications for scholarships from pupils belonging to communities backward in education, the amount may be utilized for awarding a few more scholarships in the several grades of education retaining the original rate of scholarships, as detailed in the appended statement. Government agree with the In-spector-General of Education that instead of extending the tenure of the existing Backward Classes Scholarships with the additional allotment, the number of scholarships may be increased retaining the present rates and tenure of ten months so that the scheme may benefit as large a number as possible. they are accordingly pleased to approve of his proposal. the same principle will be adopted in respect of Backward Classes scholarships in the University also as proposed by the University Council.
  • Government are also pleased to direct that as the share of the Depressed Classes in the allotment for the Backward Class scholarships has been withdrawn and included in the separate Depressed Classes Scholarships Fund, under Government Order No. E. 6220-1Edn. 457-22-1, dated the 21st May 1923, the sum of Rs. 4,820 due to the Depressed Classes pupils out of the additional grant of Rs. 17,000 may also be similarly separated and added to the Depressed Classes Scholarship Fund.

The Inspector-General of Education is requested to submit proposals for the utilization of this amount for awarding scholarships to pupils for the Depressed Classes at the rates approved in Government Order dated 21st May 1923.

K. MATTHAN
Chief secretary to Government

Document No. (3)

Representation of Indian Christian and other Depressed Classes in the Legislative Council Order No. P. 5252-61- Legis. 13-23-34, dated 19th March, 1924 (Published on March 20, 1924 p.20 )

In Notification No. P. 2402-Legis. 13-23-2, dated the November 1923, the Government of His Highness the Maharaja have been pleased to guarantee to the interests specified below representation in the Legislative Council as noted against each:-

Mahomadans … … … 2 seats
Indian Christians … … … 1 seat
Depressed Classes … … … 1 seat

Government have also directed that if these Interests fail to secure this number through the Representative assembly and the electorates for the return of non-official members specified in Schedule 2 of the Legi slative Council Regulation, Government will make good the difference by nominations through associations to be recognized for the purpose.

On an examination of the results of the elections, Government observe that while the Mahomadans have secured two seats in the Legislative Council, the other two communities have failed to secure any. In these circumstances, it is necessary for Government to provide for the representation of these communities by nomination of members returned through associations. Government is accordingly pleased to recognize the Indian Christian Association, Bangalore the Catholic Association, Bangalore and the Mysore Indian Christian Education fund Association, Bangalore, for the purpose of returning a member representing Indian Christian and the Panchama Association, Chitaldrug, the Adi Dravida Abhivriddi Sangam, Bangalore, and the Adi Dravida Jana Sabha, Kolar Gold fields for the purpose of returning a member representing the Depressed Classes. Each of the two groups of Associations recognized above will form a single constituency and the voters will be those whose names are contained in the list published in the Mysore Gazette, in connection with the Representative Assembly elections. the names of such of the persons as have ceased to be members since then will be removed and the names of those who have joined since then will be added. these omissions and additions must be sent to the Secretary, Legislative Council by the Secretaries of the associations concerned so as to reach him not later than the 25th March 1924.

the elections will be held under the rules published in Notification No. P. 4963-Legis. 13-23-30, dated 8th March 1924, except that the voting will not be by post but will be by ballot in the offices of each of the Associations referred to above. the Deputy Commissioners of the Districts concerned are requested to depute suitable officers to conduct the elections. the result of the election in each office will be communicated to the Secretary, Legislative Council who will consolidate them and submit them to the Government for publication in the Gazette.

Members of the Associations who desire to stand for election will notify their intention to the Secretary, Legislative Council, so as to reach him not later than the 25th March 1924. the elections will be held on the 29th March 1924.

B. NAGAPPA
Secretary to Government
Local and Legislative Departments.
