Document No. (3)

Directs that the community "Adikarnataka Balagi"and "Adikarnataka Edagai" may be shown under the head Adikarnataka in the Census.

READ-

  1. Government Order No. G. 2553-608-G.M.66-65-1, dated 23rd October 1925, directing the discontinuance of the use of the term "Holaya" or "Panchama"in official documents and the substitution therefore of the terms "Adidravida"and "Adikarnataka"for the Tamilian and the Kannadiga members of this community separately.
  2. Government Order No. G. 242-360- Census 32-39-5, dated 9th July 1940, directing that the two communities the "Holayas"and "Madigas"should be separately tabulated and enumerated as Adikarnataka "Balagai"( Right hand) and Adikarnataka "Edagai"( Left hand) respectively in the ensuing Census.
  3. Letter No. 4904-41-42, dated 14-16th February 1942, from the Census Superintendent in Mysore, Bangalore, stating that in the circumstances explained, the community may be shown under the common head "Adikarnataka"as in the past.

Document No. (4)

Order No. G. 10112-72-Census 24-41-2, dated Bangalore, the 31st March, 1942

The census Superintendent reports that though some persons have returned themselves as “Adikarnataka Balagai” and some others “Adikarnataka Edagai” most of the persons have merely returned themselves as “Adikarnatakas”. He accordingly proposes that the community may be shown under the common head “Adikarnataka” as in the past. In the circumstances, Government is pleased to approve the proposal of the Census Superintendent.

V. VEDAVYASACHARYA,
