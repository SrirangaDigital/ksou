  2. Student who once fails to secure promotion from one class to another will be given reduced scholarships, the reduction amounting to not less than one-third of the ordinary value of the scholarships according to the manner in which the candidates have acquitted themelves in their examination. Ordinarily Student who fails twice will be eligible for a scholarship and in no case student who has failed thrice.
  3. As far as possible, scholar under this scheme will be required to live in a recognized boarding establishment whether Government or Aided where such exists and the scholarship due to them will be handed over to the managers of the institutions to be expended on their behalf, proper accounts being maintained in all cases by the said managers.
  4. With a view to inculcate habits of tidiness among the pupils of Depressed Classes:
    (a)
    the scholarship amount of the students in Upper primary and Middle School classes will be reduced by annas 8 per mensem and the sum so reduced ( Rs. 5 for 10 months ) will be utilized for the supply of clothing to the scholars. Similarly the whole of the scholarships in the Primary I and II classes at 0-8-0 per mensem will be utilized for the supply of clothing and it will not be paid in cash, while the pupils of the High School and higher classes are expected to provide themselves with decent clothing out of their scholarship.
    (b)
    In the case of non-scholarship holders, a sum not exceeding Rs.5 each will, where necessary, be paid towards the clothing of the student for which purpose a lump sum provision of Rs. 2,000 has been provided during the year.
  5. In all the above cases, the Circle Inspectors and Order Inspectors, as the case may be, will draw the clothing allowance, i.e., Rs. 5 each in advance, and order out a set of uniform clothing for each student, the cost being within the clothing allowance sanctioned, and distributing for each student. Any change remaining after this supply may be paid to them. In the case of scholarship holders, the signature of the students should be taken in the salary acquittance roll of the school, both for the supply of cloth, and the payment of cash of scholarship holders and for the supply of clothing in the case of non-scholarship holders. Further instructions regarding this supply and distribution will issue shortly.
  6. Applications in the prescribed form, either printed or manuscript, from students of Primary and Middle schools must, in the case of boys, be submitted to the District Inspectors through their Head Masters and from those of High Schools to the Circle Inspectors not later than 1st July 1923. the applications of girls of all grades whether reading in boys or girls Schools should however be sent to the Inspectors not later than the same date. those of the U.E. students should be submitted to the Inspector- general of Education through the Heads of the Institutions in which the candidates are studying, not later than 7th July 1923. the District Inspectors will consolidate the applications received and submit the same to the Circle Inspectors concerned not later than 7th July 1923 and at the same time award scholarship of the primary grade. the arrangements of names recommended by the Inspectors Officer referred to above should be according to standards and in the order of preference full information being furnished as in the application form.

N.B.:- Applications submitted direct to the Head office by the students or their guardians as well as those that are received after the specified dates will not be considered.

ANNEXURE IV RULES REGARDING ASSOCIATIONS REPRESENTATING SPECIAL INTERESTS

Under provison (b) to section 3 (3) of the Mysore Representative Assembly Regulation XVIII of 1923, the following rules are prescribed as regards associations representing special interests and desirous of deputing members to the Representative Assembly:

  1. The association must have been formed for the furtherance of the special interest concerned or for its general advancement.
  2. It shall be registered under the Mysore Societies Registration Regulation.
  3. When any special interest has an association with branches or has two or more such associations, two or more of them may, when necessary, be grouped together by Government for the election of the member or members to be returned by the interest.
  4. the number of members on the roll of the association or associations shall not be less than on hundred.
    Provided that Government may, for special reasons, allow an association containing less than the above minimum number of members to depute a member to the assembly.
  5. Meetings if the general body or the Managing Committee shall be held at least twice a year and minutes of the meetings shall be kept with the records of the association. On a requisition from the Deputy Commissioner, the association shall submit an annual statement of the number of meetings held and the number of members present.
  6. Bye-laws of the association and all subsequent changes, as and when they are made, shall be submitted to Government.
  7. the privilege of representation given to an association shall be liable to be revoked during the term of an assembly if the association ceases to exist or infringes any of the conditions.

Notification No. P.2395-Legis. 12-23-2, dated the 7th November, 1923

In exercise of the powers vested in them under proviso (c) to Section 3 (3), Section 3(1) and Section 6 (2) (c) of the Mysore Representative Assembly Regulation, XVIII of 1923, the Government of His Highness the Maharaja are pleased to make the following rules for the representation of Minorities in the Representative Assembly

  1. The number of representatives of Minorities shall be 35 and of these 25 will be returned by election by associations to be recog- nized for the purpose and 10 will be nominated by Government for the purpose of removing inequalities, if any, of representation:
    Provided that, should there be any vacancies in the number to be elected by in associations, they may also be filled by nomination by Government.
  2. The associations recognized by Government for representing minorities shall be Governed by the rules framed by Government in this behalf under proviso ( c) to Section 3 (3) of the regulation and31 embodied in paragraph 23 of G.O. No. 1367-1426-C. B. 100-23-1, dated the 27th October 1923 (reproduced as an annexure to this noti- fication).
