ANNEXURE IV RULES REGARDING ASSOCIATIONS REPRESENTATING SPECIAL INTERESTS

Under provison (b) to section 3 (3) of the Mysore Representative Assembly Regulation XVIII of 1923, the following rules are prescribed as regards associations representing special interests and desirous of deputing members to the Representative Assembly:

  1. The association must have been formed for the furtherance of the special interest concerned or for its general advancement.
  2. It shall be registered under the Mysore Societies Registration Regulation.
  3. When any special interest has an association with branches or has two or more such associations, two or more of them may, when necessary, be grouped together by Government for the election of the member or members to be returned by the interest.
  4. the number of members on the roll of the association or associations shall not be less than on hundred.
    Provided that Government may, for special reasons, allow an association containing less than the above minimum number of members to depute a member to the assembly.
  5. Meetings if the general body or the Managing Committee shall be held at least twice a year and minutes of the meetings shall be kept with the records of the association. On a requisition from the Deputy Commissioner, the association shall submit an annual statement of the number of meetings held and the number of members present.
  6. Bye-laws of the association and all subsequent changes, as and when they are made, shall be submitted to Government.
  7. the privilege of representation given to an association shall be liable to be revoked during the term of an assembly if the association ceases to exist or infringes any of the conditions.

  • Every person shall be entitled to have his name registered on the electoral roll of constituency who has the qualifications prescribed for an elector of that constituency and who is not subject to any of the disqualifications herein after set out viz.,-
    1. Who is not a subject of His Highness the Maharaja by birth or domicile and who has not resided in the Mysore State for six months prior to the preparation of the electoral roll:
      Provided that, in the case of persons possessing the prescribed property qualifications referred to in Rules 2 and 3 infra, residence in the Civil and Military Station, Bangalore, shall not disqualify for voting.
      Note- Subject of His Highness the Maharaja by domicile from purpose of the purposes of the rule means one who has resided in the Mysore State for a period of not less than five years.
      Provided also that in the case of electors of representatives of special interests the Government may grant exemption from the operation of this rule in special cases.
    2. Who is under 21 years of age; or
    3. Who is of unsound mind; or
    4. Who is an undischarged insolvent or bankrupt; or
    5. Who has been sentenced by a Criminal Court to imprisonment for six months or more, or convicted of an offence punishable with imprisonment for seven years or more; or
    6. Who is a dismissed Government servant
      Provided that the Vth and VIth grounds of disqualification shall cease to operate from ten years after the dated of release or dismissal or earlier under an order of Government.
  • 2. The following persons are qualified as electors in a rural constituency:-
    1. Every person-
      1. Who is the registered occupant of land situated in such constituency and assessed to land revenue of not less than Rs. 25 per annum payable to Government, or
      2. Who is a kadim tenant paying an annual rent of not less than Rs. 25 per annum on account of land held by him in the constituency to the holder of an alienated village to which the provisions of Chapters VIII to X of the Land Revenue Code have been applied, or
      3. Who pays annually Mohatarfa or Municipal tax of not less than Rs. 5 in the constituency
    2. Every person who is the owner of one or more entire Inam villages with a total beriz of Rs. 125 per annum situated in the constituency and who ordinarily resides I the constituency.
    3. Every graduate of a University who ordinarily resides in the constituency.
    4. Every person who is a retired and pensioned officer (whether commissioned or non-commissioned) of the Mysore State Troops and who resides in the constituency.
    5. Every person paying income-tax to Government and residing in the constituency.

    Note- (1) if property is held or payments are made jointly by members of a joint or partnership, the family or partnership shall be adopted as a unit for deciding whether the qualification exists, and the vote shall be exercised, in the case of a Hindu joint family by the manager thereof, or the member authorized by a majority of the family, and in other cases by the member or partner authorized in that behalf by a majority of the family or the partnership concerned.

    Explanation: - "Partnership" includes a company, firm, and association, body of two or more guardians or trustees or the joint pattadars or other body possessing joint rights.

    Note (2) - Persons residing in the Jaghirs of Yelandur and Sringeri and also who satisfy the conditions under (a) (ii) and (iii) and (2) (c) and (d) will be deemed qualified as electors in these two constituencies.

  • The following persons are qualified as electors in an urban constituency:-
    1. Every person who pays in the constituency such amount of municipal tax, land revenue or mohatarfa as will qualify him to be entered in the list of voters for municipal elections in the constituency;
    2. Every graduate of a University who ordinarily resides in the constituency;
    3. Every person who is a retired and pensioned officer (whether commissioned or non-commissioned) of the Mysore State Troops and who resides in the constituency;
    4. Every person paying income-tax to Government and residing in the constituency;
  • The persons qualified as electors of representatives of special interests are those described in the third column of the Annexure to this Notification.
  • The persons qualified as electors of representatives of minorities through associations recognized as such are the members of such associations.

PART I PREPARATION AND PUBLICATION OF ELECTORAL ROLLS

  • An electoral roll shall be prepared in English and Kannada for every constituency in which shall be entered the names of all persons appearing to be entitled to be registered as electors for that constituency under the rules published with Notification No. P. 2327- LEGIS. 12-23-4, dated 7th November 1923.
  • In the case of all rural constituencies except the Kolar Gold Fields Sanitary Board area, the electoral rolls shall be prepared by the Amildar or the Deputy Amildar having jurisdiction over the area. In the case of the Kolas Gold Fields Sanitary Board area and the Bangalore and Mysore urban constituencies, the rolls shall be prepared by the Vice-President of the Sanitary Board or of the Municipal Council as the case may be, or in the absence of a Vice-President by the Chief Officer of the Municipality. In the case of all other urban areas the electoral rolls shall be prepared by the Presidents of the respective Municipal Councils.
  • The rolls prepared under rule 1, will be published by the Government or by such other authority as they may appoint in that behalf in the official Gazette and in such other manner as may be approved by Government.
  • Within a period of 3 weeks after the publication of the electoral roll of any constituency, any person whose name is not entered in the roll or any person whose name is entered in the roll and who objects to the inclusion of his name or of the name of any other person in the roll, may prefer a claim or objection to the revising authority hereunder described. PART II CONDUCT OF ELECTIONS A- Rural Constituencies

  • Whenever a general election to the Representative Assembly or an election to fill up a casual vacancy is to be held the Government shall fix a date and shall by notification in the Official Gazette not less than six weeks previous to the date fixed for the election and in such other manner as they may decide give due publicity of such date.
  • Such of the eligible persons as wish to stand for election in any rural constituency shall furnish their names in writing to the Amildar or Deputy Amildar or the Vice-President of the Kolar Gold fields Sanitary Board as the case may be within such time as may be fixed by Government.
  • The said officer shall, if he is satisfied that such persons are eligible to stand for election, prepare a list of such candidates which shall be posted up under his signature in his office. A copy of the list shall also be published in the Mysore Gazette by the Deputy Commissioner of the district in which the constituency is situated or by the President of the Kolar Gold Fields Sanitary Board area in the case of the Kolar Gold Fields Sanitary Board constituency.
    1. If the number of candidates standing for election is equal to the number of vacancies, all such candidates shall be declared duly elected.
    2. If the number of candidates is less than the number of vacancies, all such candidates shall be declared duly elected and the Deputy Commissioner shall, by a notification in the Official candidates for the remaining vacancy or vacancies and have the election conducted in accordance with these rules.
  • If the number of candidates is more than the number of vacancies, a poll shall be taken.
  • There may be more than one polling centre in each constituency and the polling shall take place at each center and at such hours as may be notified by the Deputy Commissioner and it shall be conducted by an amildar or Deputy Amildar or the Vice-President of the Kolar Gold Fields Sanitary Board or such other person as may be nominated by the Deputy Commissioner or the President of the Kolar Gold Fields Sanitary Board in this behalf. It will also be open to the deputy Commissioner or the President to nominate one or two nonofficials who may be associated with the polling officer for assisting him in conducting the poll.
  • Every voter is entitled to give as many votes as there are vacancies on the constituency, provided that not more than one vote may be given to the same candidate. A vote for any person whose name is not included in the list of candidates published under Rule 3 shall not be counted.
  • Voters must vote in person.
    1. The vote shall proceed to a place screened from observation which shall be provided by the polling officer and there mark his vote on the voting paper in accordance with the instruction contained therein.
    2. The instructions which will be in Kannada should be clearly explained to any elector requiring such explanation.
    3. If an elector is unable to read or write or is, by reason of blindness or other physical defect, incapacitated from recording his vote as required by rule, the polling officer may assist him, if so desired, in marking the vote on the voting paper and a record of the same shall be made on the voting paper and attested by the polling officer.
    4. The voting paper should be folded and closed and put into the voting box by the voter himself.
    5. Any vote which is not recorded in accordance with the foregoing instructions shall be declared invalid.
  • The ballot box shall be so constructed that the ballot paper can be introduced but not extracted without the box being unlocked, and it shall be securely sealed by the polling officer with his seal.
  • The polling officer shall satisfy himself as to the identity of the persons tendering votes and may refuse the vote of any person who declines to answer any reasonable question put to him for this purpose or whose identity is not established to his satisfaction. He is also responsible for seeing that the votes are recorded with absolute secrecy; that no person who is not entitled to vote is admitted near the voting table and that persons who have recorded their votes leave the place immediately.
  • After the close of the poll, the polling officer shall forthwith have the voting papers scrutinized and the votes counted and announce the results, provided that where this is not practicable or the ballot box has to be sent to taluk or other head quarters for counting, the polling officer shall have the box sealed with his own seal as well as those of such of the candidates or their agents as may wish to do so and arrange for their safe custody until such time as the box is dispatched to the telling officer at such headquarters.
    1. The box aforesaid shall be opened in a place to which candidates or their agents shall have access,.
    2. The votes shall be scrutinized and those that are valid counted by such persons as the polling officer appoints in this behalf.
    3. If there is an equal number of votes in favour of each of two or more candidates, and the addition of one vote will entitle any of the candidates to be declared elected, the determination of the person or persons to whom such one additional vote shall be deemed to have been given shall be made by lot to be drawn in the presence of the polling officer and in such manner as he may deem fit.
  • The results of every election shall be certified by the polling officer to the Deputy Commissioner or the President of the Kolar gold fields Sanitary Board, as the case may be, who shall report the same to Government for being notified in the Mysore Gazette.

B- Urban Constituencies

The rules relating to the conduct of elections in rural constituencies shall apply to urban constituencies with the following modifications:-

  1. The authority to whom candidates should notify their intension to stand for election shall be the vice-president or the Chief Officer of the Municipal area concerned in the case of the Bangalore and Mysore urban constituencies and the President in the case of other urban constituencies.
  2. The polling officer shall be the President, Vice-President or Chief Officer as the case may be. In case there is more than one polling station, the President may depute a member or members of the council as polling officer or officers.
  3. The certified results of the elections shall be sent to the President who will report the same to the Government for being notified in the Gazette.

C- Associations representing Special interests and Minorities

The election of a member by an association recognized by Government for the representation of a special interest or a minority under the rules framed by them on that behalf shall be made at a general meeting at which not less than 25 percent of the number of members of the association or 50 members whichever is less, are present. The roll of membership of the association referred to in Rule (d) of the rules laid down in Para 23 of G.O. no. 1367-1426-C. B. 100-23-1, dated the 27th October 1923 shall be conclusive evidence of the membership of the association.

When any special interest or minority is represented through more than one association or through an association with branches which are grouped together by Government under rule (c) of the rules referred to above for the election of a member or members to be returned for the representation of the special interest or minority concerned, Government may appoint a special officer for preparing the roll of electors and the conduct of elections. Such special officer shall have power, subject to the general or special orders of Government, to arrange for the poll and declare the results.

In the case of the constituencies formed for the representation of the Mysore University and legal interests, the Registrar of the Mysore University and the Registrar of the Mysore chief Court shall be polling officers of the constituencies concerned and the Vice- chancellor and the Chief Judge or such other person as may be appointed by them shall be the appellate authority in respect of all matters connected with the conduct of elections.

Government may by a special order permit election through postal voting in the case of any special interest or group of Associations which in the opinion of Government Cannot hold the election at a meeting.

  • A person shall be deemed to commit a corrupt practice within the meaning of these rules:-
    1. Who with a view to induce any voter to give or to refrain from giving a vote in favour of any candidate offers or gives any valuable consideration or holds out any promise of individual profit or holds out any threat or injury to any person.
    2. Who himself or through his agent or at this expense feeds or entertains his voters shortly before or after the poll, or pays the expenses of travel or hired conveyance, in order to induce them to give votes in his favour: or
    3. Who gives, procures, or abets the giving of a vote in the name of a voter who not person is giving such vote.
    Explanation-A corrupt practice shall be deemed to be committed by a candidate if it committed with his knowledge and consent or by a person who is acting under the general or special authority of such candidate with reference to the election. A 'promise of individual profit'' includes a promise for the benefit of the person himself or of any one in whom he is interested.
  • Every petition against the return of any candidate at an election to the Assembly on the ground of corrupt practice or on the ground of any material irregularity shall be presented in writing to the Deputy Commissioner by any person who was a candidate at the election or by not less than twenty five voters within one week after the day on which the results of election was declared
  • The petition shall specify the acts which objector or objectors allege as amounting to corrupt practice or material irregularity and affecting the result of the election be supported by a statement made on oath by each objector. Provided that on sufficient cause being shown, the limit of one week prescribed by the n rule may be extended by the Deputy Commissioner.
  • Every objections petition shall be accompanied by a deposit of Rs. 100 (one hundred) as security for all costs, charges and expenses which may become payable by the applicant.
    1. The petition shall be posted for hearing and notice thereof shall be given to the candidate whose election has been objected to. The Deputy Commissioner after receiving the explanation of the latter shall held a summary enquiry as defined in the land Revenue Code into the allegations and record a finding thereon. If he finds that persons not included in the list of voters have been allowed to vote, he will himself modify the elections by excluding the votes of persons not authorized to vote. If he finds that a candidate is guilty of corrupt practices, he shall order such elections to be invalid and declare the person who has secured the next highest number of votes as the candidate duly elected. An appeal shall lie to Government against the decision of the Deputy Commissioner; and the order of Government will be final.
    2. Where the enquiry does not clearly establish the objections alleged, but the Deputy Commissioner doubts, the validity of elections the records together with his openion will be submitted for orders of Government.
    3. After the cases are thus finally disposed of the results of election will be published by the Government in the Mysore Gazette.

Explanation- A corrupt practice shall be deemed to be committed by a candidate if it is committed with his knowledge or consent or by a person who is acting under the general or special authority of such candidate with reference to the election. "A promise of individual profit" includes a promise for the benefit of the person himself or of any one in whom he is interested.
  • Every petition against the return of any candidate at an elec- tion for the Legislative Council on the ground of corrupt practice or on the ground of any material irregularity shall be made in writing signed by any person who was a candidate at the election or by not less than 25 voters and shall be presented within one week after the date on which the result of election was declared, to the District Judge having jurisdiction over the area in which the election has been or should have been held:
    Provided that on sufficient cause being shown the limit of one week prescribed by these rules may be extended by the District Judge.
  • The petition shall specify the acts which the objector or objec- tors allege as amounting to corrupt practice or material irregularity and affecting the result of the election and which he or they are pre- pared to substantiate and on presentation be supported by a state- ment made on oath by each objector.
  • Every objection petition shall be accompanied by a deposit of Rs. 100 as security for all costs, charges and expenses which may become payable by the petitioner or petitioners.
    • The District Judge may, after such enquiry as he deems necessary, pass an order confirming or amending the declared result of the election or setting the election aside. For the purpose of the said enquiry, the District Judge may summon or enforce the attend- ance of witness and compel them to give evidence as if he were a civil court and he may also direct, by whom the whole or any part of the costs of any such enquiry shall be paid and such costs if any exceed the sum of Rs. 100 paid at the time of the presentation of the petition shall be recoverable as if they had been awarded in a suit under the code of Civil procedure. The decision or order of the dis- trict judge shall be final. If he sets aside the election, necessary steps shall be taken for holding a fresh election.
      1. The district Judge if satisfied that a candidate has within the meaning of section 2 committed any corrupt practice for the pur- pose of election, shall set aside the election of such candidate if he has been elected.
      2. If in any case to which clause (a) does not apply the validity of the election os in dispute between two or more candidates, the61 district judge shall, after scrutiny and computation of the votes recorded in favour of each such candidate, declare the candidate who has won the greatest number of votes to have been duly elected: provided that fro the purpose of such computation no vote shall be reckoned as valid of the District Judge finds that any corrupt practice was committed by any person known or unknown in giving or obtaining it.
    • If the validity if the election is questioned or disputed only on the ground of an error by the officer or officers charged with carrying out the rules relating to the conduct of elections or of an irregularity or informality not corruptly caused, the district judge shall not set aside the election.
